Feature Styles

Bangalow Style

A Bangalow style house is typically a single-story dwelling characterized by its low-pitched roof with wide eaves, often supported by decorative brackets.This architectural style emphasizes open floor plans, natural materials, and a seamless blend with outdoor spaces, reflecting a relaxed, tropical lifestyle.

Clacical Style

A classical style house draws inspiration from ancient Greek and Roman architecture, featuring symmetrical proportions, grand columns, and ornate detailing. These homes often have a formal layout with a central entrance, balanced facade, and sometimes include pediments, pilasters, and cornices to add elegance.

Luxury Style

Luxury style houses are characterized by their opulence, extravagance, and high-quality materials and finishes. These homes often boast spacious layouts with expansive rooms, lavish amenities such as home theaters, wine cellars, and spa-like bathrooms, and state-of-the-art technology integrated throughout. Architectural features may include grand entranceways, soaring ceilings, intricate moldings, and custom-designed fixtures.

Cottage Style

A Cottage style house is cozy and charming, often characterized by a modest size, steeply pitched roofs, and asymmetrical facades. Originating from rural England, these homes typically feature quaint details such as decorative trim, shutters, and flower boxes, evoking a sense of nostalgia and warmth. Inside, Cottage style houses often have open floor plans, beamed ceilings, and intimate living spaces, emphasizing comfort and coziness.

Carftsman Style

Craftsman style houses are characterized by their emphasis on craftsmanship, natural materials, and simplicity of design. Originating in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, these homes typically feature low-pitched roofs with wide eaves, exposed rafters, and decorative beams. They often boast front porches with tapered columns or pillars, as well as extensive use of wood, stone, and earthy colors.

Modern Style

A Modern style house is characterized by its clean lines, minimalist design, and emphasis on functionality and simplicity. Originating in the early to mid-20th century, modern homes often feature open floor plans, large windows, and a focus on geometric shapes and asymmetry. They incorporate innovative materials such as concrete, steel, and glass, and often prioritize energy efficiency and sustainable design principles.

Feature collections

One Story House

A one-story house is a single-level dwelling, offering convenience and accessibility with all living spaces, bedrooms, and amenities situated on a single floor. This layout eliminates the need for stairs, making it ideal for individuals seeking ease of movement and a practical living environment.

Two Story House

A two-story house features two levels of living space, with bedrooms, bathrooms, and additional rooms situated on the upper floor, while common areas like the living room, kitchen, and dining area are typically located on the main floor. This layout provides separation between public and private spaces, offering versatility and functionality.

Two Bedroom House

A two-bedroom house offers compact living with two bedrooms, ideal for small families, couples, or individuals seeking a balance between space and affordability. These homes typically feature a functional layout with a living area, kitchen, and bathroom, providing comfort and convenience within a modest footprint.

Five Bedroom House

A five-bedroom house offers ample space for large families or those needing extra rooms for guests, offices, or hobbies. These homes often boast a spacious layout with multiple living areas, a gourmet kitchen, and several bathrooms, providing versatility and comfort for a variety of lifestyles and needs.

Walkout Basement House

A walkout basement house features a lower level that is partially above ground, allowing for direct access to the outdoors. This layout provides additional living space, often utilized as recreational areas, guest quarters, or home offices, while still offering natural light and easy access to outdoor amenities like patios or gardens.

Garage Plane House

A garage plane house integrates a spacious garage area seamlessly into the home's design. This layout typically features a main living area on one level, with the garage positioned below or adjacent to the living spaces. It offers convenience, ample storage for vehicles, and potential for additional living or recreational space.
